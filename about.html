Liz Brown // Design Research and Strategy

Liz Brown

My journey as a Designer Researcher and UX Leader is shaped by a Master’s in HCI from Michigan and, Bachelor’s in Psychology from Pittsburgh, and immensely influenced by seminal research studies spanning concepts such as: operate conditioning, authority, companionship, information processing, conformity, group think, boredom, Fitt’s Law, Moore’s Law, typography, and color theory – providing a strong theoretical foundation for my human-centered design practice.

As a lifelong learner, I frequently draw inspiration and knowledge from a wide array of resources. From the practical foundations of the Design Dictionary to the critical insights of Sara Ahmed and Judith Butler, the philosophical inquiries of Baruch Spinoza, the cultural explorations of Joan Morgan, the strategic product thinking of Teresa Torres, the adaptable wisdom of Bruce Lee, the technical depth of the Mozilla Developer Network, and the diverse perspectives of the 1440 Newsletter.

When I step away from research and design and in my free time at home in Richmond, VA, USA, I enjoy watching films and writing screenplays. I find focus and strategy in playing chess. I sight see around my neighborhood while listening to music. I dive into imaginative worlds through books on my Kindle and explore the logic of code while programming web apps. However, over and above all, I value connecting with my family and friends.
